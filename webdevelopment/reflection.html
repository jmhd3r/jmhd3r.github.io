Reflection

Academics

Through my studies in Mathematics, Engineering, and Industrial Engineering at the University of Missouri, I’ve learned how to think critically, identify systems, and improve how they function. These disciplines have shaped my approach to problem-solving and strengthened the way I organize and analyze information.

Professional Experience

In professional settings like the Mizzou Athletics ticket office and Brown & Root, I’ve gained firsthand experience managing people, time, and communication. I learned how vital teamwork and adaptability are when facing complex or time-sensitive challenges, and how structure makes creativity more effective.

Personal Growth

Outside of academics and work, I’ve developed a love for art and detail through pyrography. That hobby allows me to slow down and express ideas visually, balancing the analytical side of my personality. My creative projects remind me to value patience and imperfection—skills that transfer into every area of life.

Future Goals

Looking ahead, I plan to pursue project or operations management roles that combine organization, design, and communication. I want to build systems that help people work more efficiently while keeping balance and creativity in focus. This portfolio marks an important step toward those goals.
